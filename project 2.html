Learn and improve through interaction with the environment (embodiment) Learn quickly from large amounts of data Have memory-based exemplar storage and retrieval capacities Have parameters to represent short and long term memory, age, forgetting, etc. Padgham & Winikoff (2005) agree that an intelligent agent is situated in an environment and responds (in a timely, though not necessarily real-time) manner to environment changes.
